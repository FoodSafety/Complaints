Food Complaint

Privacy and security statement

In this privacy and security statement, ëQueensland Healthí means the State of Queensland acting through Queensland Health.

1. Personal information

Queensland Health is committed to protecting the privacy of our website users. We understand and appreciate that visitors to, and users of, our website, are concerned about their privacy and the confidentiality and security of any personal information they provide to us.

Personal information collected, held, stored, used and disclosed by Queensland Healthís Food Complaint website is handled in accordance with the Information Privacy Act 2009 (Qld). Personal information means information or an opinion, including information or an opinion forming part of a database, whether true or not, and whether recorded in a material form or not, about an individual whose identity is apparent, or can reasonably be ascertained, from the information or opinion.

The purpose of the Food Complaint website is to provide an online interface to register a complaint about a food safety issue, e.g., food poisoning, poor food handling and hygiene, food business cleanliness, foreign matter found in food or incorrect food labelling.

2. Security

We take strong precautions to protect your data from loss, unauthorised access, use, modification or disclosure, and against misuse.

All information is stored within Australia using secure data servers which meet all the stringent security compliance requirements of the Queensland Government.

3. Website monitoring and cookies

When you visit the Food Complaint website, our internet service and analytics provider/s make a record of your visit and log the following information for statistical purposes only:

  • your internet web browser, computer platform and screen resolution; and
  • your traffic patterns through our site, including
    • the date and time of your visit;
    • the pages and documents accessed;
    • the website you visited before ours; and
    • your server address.

This website uses ëcookiesí. A cookie is a small text file stored on your computer by your web browser which allows us to store your preferences and record information about your visit. We use cookies to record usage statistics for this site, such as the number of visits, assets viewed, browser type, and the way the site is navigated. In using these cookies, we do not retrieve or record any personal information such as names or email addresses.

The anonymous statistical data we collect may be aggregated and used in broader statistical analysis or to assist in making your website experience easier and more efficient.

No attempt is, or will be, made to identify users or their browsing activities except, in the unlikely event of an investigation, where a law enforcement agency may exercise a warrant to inspect website activity logs.

4. Website correspondence and public records

All correspondence sent to this website will constitute public records and will be retained as required by the Public Records Act 2002 (Qld) and other relevant legislation.

Your contact details will only be provided to other agencies as needed to investigate your complaint and in accordance with our Privacy Statement. Under the Act, food safety complaints may be referred to one or more of the following agencies:

  • Local council;
  • Public Health Unit (Queensland Health);
  • Food Safety Standards and Regulation (Queensland Health);
  • Safe Food Production Queensland;
  • Another Queensland Government agency;
  • Interstate government agency;
  • Australian Government; and
  • New Zealand Government;

5. Email and feedback information

Your name and email details will not be added to any other mailing list, nor will we disclose these details to third parties without your consent, unless the disclosure is authorised or required by or under a law.

Email messages between yourself and Queensland Health may be monitored by our website support team for website trouble shooting and maintenance purposes.

Although best endeavours will be used, Queensland Health does not guarantee to reply to all email correspondence received via this site. Where appropriate, emails may be forwarded internally to other business areas within the Department of Health, or to an appropriate Hospital and Health Service for direct response.

6. Links to other websites

Where this website contains links to other websites, Queensland Health is not responsible for the privacy or security practices or the content of those websites. We recommend that you read the privacy statement of that website to familiarise yourself with its privacy practices.

7. Access to your personal information

Queensland Health supports your right to seek access to, or amendment of, personal information (including your health information) we hold about you. To find out how to apply for access to, or amendment of, your personal information, including your health information, or to learn more about how Queensland Health protects your privacy rights, please visit Health records and privacy.

1If you provide an email address that uses a web-based email service whose servers are based in another country (e.g. Hotmail or Gmail), your personal information may be transferred outside of Australia. By providing the email address, you voluntarily agree to this transfer.
